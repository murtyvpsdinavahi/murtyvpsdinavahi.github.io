Personal profile

I am Murty, an Indian national born in Kakinada, Andhra Pradesh. I was mostly raised in NOIDA, Uttar Pradesh (part of the National Capital Region of India).

My mother tongue is Telugu, and I speak English and Hindi as fluently as I speak Telugu. I can read and write all the three languages. I picked up Tamil during my 6-year stay in Chennai.

I am recognised as a medical practitioner by Medical Council of India, registered with Delhi Medical Council (Reg. no. 57509) and Tamil Nadu Medical Council (Reg. no. 97728). I have qualified NEET-PG (2013) and CSIR-JRF (2013). I am a member of Society for Neuroscience (SfN). I have been a past member of Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM).

Educational background

I started my formal schooling (Kindergarten) from Pine Hall, Dehradun. After attending Kindergarten at various schools in Dehradun, Vishakhapatnam, Kakinada and NOIDA, I joined Assisi Convent School, NOIDA for primary and secondary schooling. I passed secondary school with 89.6% of marks in 2004 with English, Hindi, Mathematics, Science and Social Science as subjects.

I went to Delhi Public School, Mathura Road, New Delhi for my senior school. I passed it with 90.8% marks in 2006 with English, Mathematics, Biology, Physics and Chemistry as subjects.

I began medical training as a doctor in 2006 soon after senior school. I joined the prestigious Government Stanley Medical College and Hospital, Chennai from where I pursued undergraduate medical education (M.B.B.S.) as well internship. I passed the course in March 2011 with an aggregate of 65.9% (all subjects passed in first attempt) and completed 1 year of internship (in General Medicine, General Surgery, Obstetrics and Gynecology, Pediatrics, Social and Preventive Medicine, Orthopedics, Anasthesia and Psychiatry) in April 2012.

I pursued PhD in Neuroscience from the prestigious Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru, India. I had submitted my thesis in July 2019 and defended it after peer review in January 2020. I took various ME and PhD-level courses such as Neural Signal Processing, Cellular Neurophysiology, etc. I had a CGPA of 6.6/8.0 for the course work. For my research and publications, please visit the Research and Publications pages.

Industry/hospital experience

I am a co-founder of Neurotech Labs, a start-up based in Cochin, India. At Neurotech, we are trying to make smart wearables (earphones) that help consumers to meditate and relax well. I am currently heading its neuroscience team. Our venture is funded by the Govt. of India through BIRAC.

Prior to opting for a research career, I had engaged myself briefly in clinical activity. I had worked as a resident medical officer in the Emergency and Trauma Department of in Pushpanjali Crosslay Hospital (now Max Super Speciality Hospital), Vaishali, Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh from October 2012 to January 2013.

Teaching experience

During my tenure as a PhD student at IISc, I taught EEG practical class for undergraduate students in the January-April session of 2016. I covered recording of EEG from human subjects and time frequency analysis of EEG data. This class was followed by assessment of students that included take-home assignments and viva examination.

Besides, I had also taught neuroanatomy classes to undergraduate students in August-December sessions of 2015 and 2016. I had given them an introductory lecture on neuroanatomy, followed by hands-on demonstrations of the human brain, whole and in slices.

Social service and leadership roles

I was a Founder member of student chapter of SIAM-IISc (Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics – Indian Institute of Science). I also served as its secratary and coordinated in organising lectures and workshops in applied aspects of mathematics.

I was a volunteer and executive committee member of Note Book Drive. It is a network of student volunteers from IISc and its sister institutes in Bangalore like NCBS. They work towards providing financial support and inculcating scientific attitude in school children of poor socio-economic areas of rural Bangalore. I was involved in various activities like mentoring school students, teacher training workshops, children's day celebrations, etc.

Besides these, I was a volunteer for the 4th Bangalore Cognition Workshop. I had designed posters, banners, ID cards, notebooks, etc. for the workshop. I was also involved in managing activities on stage for our lab’s module in the workshop.

At college, I worked as a volunteer (medical service) at Alamadhi (near Chennai), a rural out-patient Sunday clinic of Govt. Stanley Hospital, Chennai. This was part of the Rural Medical and Social Service League at Stanley. At this facility, interns and residents see patients from the village of Alamadhi and prescribe medicines for minor illnesses, assisted by medical students. This helps the poor villagers save a lot of commutation time, money, energy and loss of work/pay for travelling to the hospital OPD on working days.
